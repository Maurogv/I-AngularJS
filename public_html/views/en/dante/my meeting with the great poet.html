As a young man I liked to read, many were books of fiction and poetry. Numerous authors quoted and paid homage to the same poet. Aware that hardly great writers and poets could be wrong, I delayed the reading of the great poet to stay still in the shadow of the other authors, to deepen him further. They come to mind Dostoyevsky, Montale, Carducci, Foscolo, Gadda, Porta, Leopardi, Manzoni. In the second of his Canti, On the Monument of Dante,^ Giacomo Leopardi called him the one for which the meonio cantor is not alone, comparing Dante to Homer, native of Meonia, author of the Iliad and the Odyssey, the two greatest epic poems of Greek literature; Alessandro Manzoni, before the final draft of the Betrothed novel, understands having to wash clothes in the Arno.

I had studied Dante in secondary school, I remember only a few fragments of discussions during the third year on the Divine Comedy with the Italian teacher of the technical institute, with nothing left. In the first year of college I read Inferno, especially on the way to reach the polytechnic, on tram 33 which took over half an hour to cross Milan; in the evening I memorized the triplets. Wanting to know more about the origin, I read in Italian one of his first works, De Vulgari eloquentia, written in Latin, in which the Florentine poet upholds the dignity of the vernacular.
Then, I got to Convivio which surprised me from the beginning for the beauty of the language, the genius, different from all the other books I had read, both in style and in meaning, opening up a new horizon. This book is anticipation and preparation for Comedy.
