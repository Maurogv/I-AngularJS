Convivio

Convivio, impressed me, a book different from any I had read until then, those beginnings are surprising for their beauty and then hold the promise.

As the Philosopher says at the beginning of the First Philosophy, all men by nature desire to know. The reason for this can be and is that each thing, impelled by a force provided by its own nature, inclines towards its own perfection. Since knowledge is the ultimate perfection of our soul, in which resides our ultimate happiness, we are all therefore by nature subject to a desire for it.
As the Philosopher (par excellence) says, Aristotle, at the beginning of his Metaphysics, all men naturally desire to know. The reason for this desire to know is that all things, in whose nature is painted in divine providence, is inclined to its perfection, this providence, so as the science is the ultimate perfection of our soul, which is the our ultimate happiness, naturally, all are subject to this desire

Many are, however, deprived of this most noble perfection by various causes within and outside of man which remove him from the habit of knowledge.
However, this noble perfection, the natural desire to know of men, many are private to various causes, within or outside of man, which prevent him to get to the science.
Dante identifies two defects that are excusable for not arrive at perfection, body defects, deafness, muteness or similar (within of man) and the civil and family care that prevents the possibility of studying (outside of man). Others do not reach the perfection of the soul for their own are not excusable, that malice has to follow vicious pleasures (within) and laziness that does not go away those who are born in places without study facilities (outside); the first, malice, which is more worthy of blame and abomination.

This far Dante, one side has indicated the happiness of everything as natural that follows its own perfection, the other finds the causes that many away from this condition. For subtraction says:

But since man is by nature a friend of all men, and every friend is grieved by defects found in the one he loves, they who are fed at so lofty a table are not without compassion toward those whom they see grazing about on grass and acorns in animal pastures.

As mercy (pity), is the source of good deed, those who know (the few who have access to the table) liberally donate their good riches over real poor (those excluded from the table) and are almost a source of life, of which water quenches natural thirst (thirst for knowledge) named above.

It is needful to know that writing can be understood and should be expounded in four main ways.
You should know that in a text can be understood and can be explained up to four senses.
The first is termed the literal, and this is the meaning that, in poetic fables for instance, does not delve beneath the surface of the words. The next is termed the allegorical, and this meaning is concealed beneath the cloak of the fables, and is a truth hidden beneath a lovely fiction. The first way is called the literal, and this is the one that extends beyond the letters of the words that express the apparent sense, the other is called allegorical, and this is what lies beneath these tales (outward appearance), and is a truth hidden beneath a beautiful lie.
The third meaning is the moral one, and this is the meaning that teachers should seek to uncover throughout the scriptures, for their own and their pupils’ benefit. The third sense is called moral, and this is what readers have to look carefully in the Scriptures, to their utility and their disceples (pupils).
The fourth meaning is termed anagogical, that is to say beyond the senses, a spiritual meaning that overlaps with the literal, the literal text though true to what he says is to represent the eternal glory of the supernal things (divine).

It would be impossible because with regard to all that has an outside and an inside, it is impossible to arrive at the inside without first arriving at the outside; thus, given that, in what is written, the outside is always the literal meaning, it is impossible to arrive at the other meanings, especially the allegorical, without first arriving at the literal
